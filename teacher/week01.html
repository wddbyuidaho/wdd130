Instructor Preparation

Overview

The team activity for this week is from a great set of exercises from Wes Bos Javascript30. It is lesson 4: Array Cardio I. It does a great job of getting the students in and using the array methods. The readings introduce DOM manipulation and Events.

Again with the readings, encourage them also to look to other sources when things don't make sense. The book does a pretty good job, but the way it addresses some topics might be confusing to some students. Help them to remember that there are other options. Several are listed in this weeks reading in fact. The notes they turn in do not have to be all from the assigned readings.

Encourage the students also to record their questions while reading and give them to you to be addressed.

Meeting Time

For your meeting with them give an example of building some dynamic DOM and add some events. For the events portion you may consider assigning another lesson from the Javascript30 course or reviewing the concepts from it: 25: Event Capture, Propagation....

You might then do something like this dynamically built list of hikes. (Here is a link to just the html and css you could provide as a starting point to the students.

You will probably not have time to do this entire demo (pulling up a detailed view of a hike when a summary list view hike is touched)....which is fine since the last part is revisited in the Week 5 team activity. The important part is to show how to create DOM elements from a data source...and show some examples of using events.

This is also a good time to talk about issues that may arise from javascript executing before the DOM is loaded. Talk about ways to avoid those problems (onload listener, adding the script at the bottom of the body, or using the defer flag on your script element)
