Your personal Site Plan - Completed

This week along with finishing up the Dry Oar site, we need to finish up the planning for our personal site.

Website Requirements

  1. The Web site must consist of a home page and at least 2 child pages
  2. One of the child pages should incorporate a form.
  3. The Web site must be hosted on the web.
  4. Each page of your Web site must satisfy the following requirements:
    1. Significant textual content consisting of appropriate content for your audience so that they can understand the meaning and message of this site
    2. At least one image that relates to the content of the page
    3. Working links to your two child pages
    4. Be laid out and styled according to your site plan.
    5. Be visually appealing and easily readable

This week we only need to finish the planning for the personal site. We will write the HTML and CSS to build the site over the rest of the semester.

Activity Directions

  1. Create the Home wireframe

    Make sure that you have completed the Personal Site Plan part 1 and Personal Site Plan part 2: content activities. If you have completed those all that you should have left to complete your site plan is to create wireframes for the 3 pages. Review the content you prepared last week for each page that you are planning to build, then start with the Home page. Make sure you account for each block of content in your plan. Pay special attention to the header and footer as these should be on each page. Keep in mind as well the principles of good design.

  2. Create the other wireframes

    Using your Home wireframe as a starting point (remember that the header and footer should be the same across all three pages) create wireframes for your two child pages. Add all three wireframes to the Site plan.

  3. Verify

    Review each section of your site plan. Make sure that each is complete and free of spelling and grammar errors. Then publish and submit the URL of your plan to Ilearn.
